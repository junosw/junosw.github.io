Projects

Thinking about the projects I've worked on over the years, I realized I didn't have ready access in memory to anything but a thumbnail sketch of many of them. I basically remembered the elevator pitch — the blurb I would give folks I may be talking to when a particular aspect I had learned on a project became relevant to the current conversation.

Recently, I started thinking about all the details... essentially reminiscing about all of the stuff I've worked on and the great people I've worked with since graduating with my software degree. It was a fun trip down memory lane. I decided to capture it here so perhaps in the future I can come back and reminisce some more. In the meantime, it's a version of my resume in story form.

SketchDeck, 2017–current

SketchDeck is a graphic design service that leverages a web application "platform" to enable collaboration between our clients and our project managers and design teams. I was initially hired as a senior dev and two months after starting, was promoted to "Head of Product and Engineering" when the CTO left the company. This is the position I've held since 2017 and it has been a wonderful experience that I've thoroughly enjoyed...

Node & Frontend, 2017

Node had been on my radar for several years... I decided to dive into learning Node and React...

Metrics, 2016–2017

Metrics are pervasive in software... I worked on a solution to get metrics out of Java server-side applications living on Amazon EC2 instances into SignalFx...

Big Data, 2016

As the team went to production with the PrintOS Search Service... we were tasked with a new big data initiative leveraging Kafka, Apache Storm, Redshift and more...

Elasticsearch Auth, 2015

PrintOS is a cloud-based system for print service providers... my team was tasked with building the "Search Service" with Elasticsearch and a custom authorization proxy...

Rover, 2012–2015

Rover was HP's internal project name for its "Print On Demand" publishing platform... refactored into a Scala-based SOA with MongoDB and Play Framework...

Beachhead, 2011–2012

HP pivoted to build a consumer-facing desktop app for scheduled printing... essentially combining Java with Jetty and a hosted web UX...

Books On-Demand, 2009–2011

I worked at HP on an "on demand book publishing kiosk" solution... built in C#/.NET and piloted with college bookstores and Powell’s Books...

RealFlight, 2004–2009

At Knife Edge Software, I contributed to RealFlight, an R/C plane flight simulator, working primarily in C++ with DirectX libraries...
